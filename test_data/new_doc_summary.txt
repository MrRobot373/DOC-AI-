# General introduction (Page 5)

## Purpose of the tests (Page 5)
  Purpose of the tests 
  Purpose of the test is to verify the defined functionalities of the power PCB, driver PCB and controller PCB circuit, against specifications (SRS and the respective data sheets) and to identify any potential issues or areas for improvement. 
  Across the Controller PCB, Driver PCB, and Power PCB, testing emphasizes output accuracy, response speed, noise performance, and stability under lab conditions. 
  Note: Lab conditions in this document mean that the PCB is tested in the laboratory at room temperature, without extra mechanical or environmental stress, PCB is out of case and no colling system was used. 
  Controller PCB:
This board has a microcontroller, communication interfaces (CAN, LIN, debug), and I/O for relays, gate drivers, temperature sensors, current sensors, and EEPROM/FLASH. 
  Driver PCB:
This board has the gate-driver circuits and isolated driver power supplies used to drive the GaN and SiC power devices. 
  Power PCB:
This board has the main high-power stages (PFC, DCDC, OBC), the GaN/SiC power switches, current and voltage-measurement circuits, filters, DC-link parts, and discharge circuits.

## Conclusion (Page 12)
  Conclusion 
  The OBC DAB test was performed at 400 V DC input and 200 kHz. The converter achieved 800 V class output (measured 798 V); therefore, the input DC voltage and output voltage target requirements were met. However, the test has not yet reached the rated 11 kW condition. The measured currents (Iin = 4.90 A and Iout = 10.2 A) are below the required rated currents (Iin ≥ 27.5 A and Iout ≥ 13.75 A) needed to achieve 11 kW. Hence, the test is PASS for voltage verification and fail for full-power (11 kW)

## Test criteria (Page 22)
  Test criteria 
  To verify that the OBC and DCDC circuits switch between 200 kHz and 500 kHz during normal operation, and to verify that if any one gate drive fails or any one gate driver power supply fails, the unit performs a complete shutdown (stops switching and disables outputs) with no continued operation.

## Test procedures (Page 22)
  Test procedures 
  Step 1: Ultra Small Setup
• Place the fully assembled Ultra Small unit on an insulated test bench.
• Ensure the AC source, HV load, CAN tool, and oscilloscope are OFF before wiring.
• Verify the unit is mechanically secured and properly grounded. 
  Step 2: Wiring Setup
• Consider the same AC→DC charging setup:
– Connector 1 → AC input (L, N, PE) from programmable AC source
– Connector 5 → HV output (HV+, HV–) to HV load / battery simulator
– Connector 3 → CAN tool (CAN_H, CAN_L, GND) for enable and fault monitoring
Measurement setup (oscilloscope):
• Connect oscilloscope probes to measure switching frequency:
– OBC stage: probe the OBC PWM/gate test point (gate signal)
– DCDC stage: probe the DCDC PWM/gate test point (gate signal)
• Set os 
  Step 3: Start Normal Operation
• Set AC source to nominal input voltage and frequency:
– 110 Vac model: 110 Vac, 50 Hz
– 220 Vac model: 230 Vac, 50 Hz
• Turn ON AC source.
• Enable AC→DC charging via CAN.
• Apply HV load so the unit is operating in steady state. 
  Step 4: Measure OBC Switching Frequency
• Capture the OBC gate/PWM waveform on the oscilloscope.
• Record measured switching frequency.
• Confirm frequency is between 200 kHz and 500 kHz.
• Save oscilloscope screenshot as evidence. 
  Step 5: Measure DCDC Switching Frequency
• Capture the DCDC gate/PWM waveform on the oscilloscope.
• Record measured switching frequency.
• Confirm frequency is between 200 kHz and 500 kHz.
• Save oscilloscope screenshot as evidence. 
  Step 6: Gate Drive Failure Shutdown Test (Single Failure)
• Keep unit in normal operation under load.
• Introduce a single gate-drive failure on one channel (one gate drive only).
• Observe and record:
– Switching waveforms stop (OBC and DCDC gate signals go inactive)
– HV output at Connector 5 drops to safe state (no power conversion)
– CAN reports fault / unit state changes to fault/shutdown
• Save oscilloscope screenshot showing switching disabled. 
  Step 7: Gate Driver Power Supply Failure Shutdown Test (Single Failure)
• Restore unit to normal operation (reset/clear fault as per standard method).
• Introduce failure of one gate-driver power supply (remove/disable driver supply).
• Observe and record:
– Switching waveforms stop (OBC and DCDC gate signals go inactive)
– HV output at Connector 5 disabled / drops to safe state
– CAN reports fault / unit enters shutdown
• Save oscilloscope screenshot showing switching disabled. 
  Step 8: Power-Down Sequence
• Disable operation via CAN.
• Reduce AC input to 0 Vac and turn OFF AC source.
• Turn OFF HV load/battery simulator.
• Disconnect oscilloscope and CAN connections.

# Dielectric Withstand and Insulation Resistance Verification (Page 25)
  Dielectric Withstand and Insulation Resistance Verification 

## Test criteria (Page 25)
  Test criteria 
  To verify the unit passes isolation and insulation by withstanding 2.5 kVrms between HV+/HV− and LV GND, achieving insulation resistance of ≥20 MΩ (HV to LV GND) and ≥10 MΩ (AC L/N to Body, AC L/N to HV+, AC L/N to HV−), and confirming LV GND is shorted to the Body, with correct insulation distances for basic (2500 V impulse) and reinforced (4000 V impulse) insulation. 
  Table 10 Expected output dielectric withstand and insulation resistance verification 

## Test procedures (Page 25)
  Test procedures 
  Step 1: Safety Setup
• Place the unit on an insulated bench and ensure the area is clear.
• Ensure the unit is powered OFF, AC source disconnected, and HV is fully discharged.
• Confirm no cables are connected to Connector 1, Connector 3, Connector 4, or Connector 5 except the test instrument leads. 
  Step 2: Equipment Setup
• Use a Hi-Pot tester for dielectric withstands (kVrms).
• Use an Insulation Resistance tester (Megger) for MΩ measurements.
• Use a DMM for LV GND-to-body short (Ω). 
  A) Dielectric Withstand Test (Hi-Pot) HV to LV GND (2.5 kVrms) 
  Step 3: Connect Hi-Pot Leads
• Connect Hi-Pot HIGH lead to Connector 5 (HV+) and Connector 5 (HV−) tied together.
• Connect Hi-Pot RETURN led to LV GND on Connector 3. 
  Step 4: Apply Withstand Voltage
• Set Hi-Pot to 2.5 kVrms.
• Set ramp time and dwell time as per your lab standard (example: ramp 5 s, dwell 60 s).
• Start the Hi-Pot test. 
  Step 5: Record Result
• Record applied voltage, leakage current, and pass/fail outcome.
• Stop the tester and discharge the DUT using the tester discharge function. 
  B) Insulation Resistance Tests (Megger) 
  Step 6: HV to LV GND (≥ 20 MΩ)
• Connect Megger HIGH lead to Connector 5 (HV+) and HV− tied together.
• Connect Megger LOW/RETURN lead to LV GND at Connector 3.
• Apply the insulation test voltage as per your standard (example: 500 Vdc or 1000 Vdc).
• Measure and record insulation resistance; must be ≥ 20 MΩ. 
  Step 7: AC L/N to Body (≥ 10 MΩ)
• Tie Connector 1 (L and N) together.
• Connect Megger HIGH lead to tied L+N at Connector 1.
• Connect Megger LOW/RETURN lead to Body/Chassis.
• Measure and record insulation resistance; must be ≥ 10 MΩ. 
  Step 8: AC L/N to HV+ (≥ 10 MΩ)
• Tie Connector 1 (L and N) together.
• Connect Megger HIGH lead to tied L+N at Connector 1.
• Connect Megger LOW/RETURN lead to Connector 5 (HV+).
• Measure and record insulation resistance; must be ≥ 10 MΩ. 
  Step 9: AC L/N to HV− (≥ 10 MΩ)
• Tie Connector 1 (L and N) together.
• Connect Megger HIGH lead to tied L+N at Connector 1.
• Connect Megger LOW/RETURN lead to Connector 5 (HV−).
• Measure and record insulation resistance; must be ≥ 10 MΩ. 
  C) LV Ground to Body Bond (Conductive Short) 
  Step 10: Bonding Check (Near 0 Ω)
• Using a DMM in resistance mode:
• Connect one probe to LV GND at Connector 3.
• Connect the other probe to Body/Chassis.
• Record resistance; it must indicate short/conductive bond (near 0 Ω). 
  D) Insulation Distance Verification (Visual/Measurement) 
  Step 11: Basic and Reinforced Distance Check
• Using calibrated calipers/creepage gauge, measure:
– Basic insulation spacing locations (target rated impulse 2500 V)
– Reinforced insulation spacing locations (target rated impulse 4000 V)
• Record measured clearance/creepage values for the defined PCB and mechanical barriers. 
  Step 12: Power-Down / Disconnect
• Ensure the DUT is discharged.
• Remove all test leads from Connector 1, Connector 3, and Connector 5.
• Attach all screenshots/printouts from Hi-Pot and Megger results to the report.
